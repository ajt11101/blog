So I was in a wedding ceremony today, the place was well - decorated, lightings, theme were all so beautiful. While I was there roaming around, two things caught my attention, one was the huge variety of good food of which I am passionate about, and the other was the staff who was serving it.

I saw that there were few children of age around 12-16 years, they were there dressed in the costumes serving food, which they can't have on the same plate and same table to whom they were serving. Even if one would have dared to do so, he would have been fired from his job or something worse, a public humiliation by the person to whom he must have been reporting. But that's okay there are certain guidelines to every job, and this must have been the agreement to which he must have signed. There is no issue in it.

What made me sad, was that these kids wouldn't have asked for this life in the wildest of there dreams, where on one side they were serving food to the children of their age, and their parents pampering them to eat one more bite, and on the other side of it, they themselves being, hopeless, pathetic, thinking what's wrong with them, they didn't asked for this life yet they were served this life. They were subjected to this struggle in this capitalistic world without them being aware of it.

In my opinion this raises a serious question - Does it really makes sense to have kids in this world if you can't afford to give them a good life ?
If no, then won't we should collaboratively whatever we can afford to do on an individual level, use that to empower the millions of homeless children who are already there. I believe this would help this world to be a better place to live in.

The wedding ceremony for any other person would have been the happy moment because of good food, family gathering and other similiar reasons, but for the kids who were serving food it would have been the happy moment, because this ocassion gave them the privelege to earn today and also the good food, but not served in the way, they would have wanted.
